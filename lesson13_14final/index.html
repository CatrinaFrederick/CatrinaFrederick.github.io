About Calaveras Big Trees National Park

Our Park was discovered in 1852, when hunter Augustus Dowd chased a bear into the forest at Calaveras, and was stopped in his tracks by the sight of trees many times more massive than any he’d ever seen. He began spreading the word of the amazing trees. Since its discovery Calaveras Big Trees National Park had became a popular tourist attraction, unfortunately, one ofits most sizable trees — the one that made Dowd’s jaw drop — was cut down for exhibition and profit shortly after its discovery.

Caclaveras Big Trees was Established as a national park in 1931, Calaveras Big Trees State Park preserves two groves of giant sequoias - the world's largest trees - in the North and South Groves. The park is a mixed-conifer forest (a variety of trees living together). In addition to the giant trees, you will find the Stanislaus River, Beaver Creek, ancient volcanic formations, and natural meadows. Trails throughout the park allow you to discover the natural beauty that has awed visitors to the area since 1852.
